Pro Travel Tips

Travel Smarter, Not Harder

Expert advice from experienced Indian travelers to make every trip safer, cheaper, and more memorable.

01

Book Flights 6–8 Weeks Early

International fares from India drop significantly 6–8 weeks ahead. Always search in incognito mode to prevent algorithmic price hikes. Compare on Google Flights, MakeMyTrip, and Skyscanner.

02

Never Skip Travel Insurance

A ₹500–₹2,000 policy can save lakhs in overseas medical emergencies. Bajaj Allianz and HDFC ERGO offer excellent India-specific plans. Always check if adventure activities are covered.

03

Carry a Forex Card

Multi-currency forex cards (HDFC, Thomas Cook, Niyo Global) save 3–5% on conversion versus airport exchange counters. Load before departure. Best deal — never exchange at airports.

04

Travel in Shoulder Season

Bali in November, Europe in October, Dubai in May. Hotels drop 40–60% and iconic sights lose their crowds entirely. Shoulder season = fewer tourists + better value + perfect weather.

05

Download Offline Maps

Google Maps and Maps.me work offline. Download the destination map before landing — a lifesaver in areas with patchy signal. Also download translation apps for non-English countries.

06

Always Pack a Power Bank

Anker and Ambrane make reliable TSA-approved 20,000 mAh power banks. Keep everything charged on long travel days. Also carry universal adapters — plug types differ globally.

07

Apply for Visas Early

Schengen visas for Europe take 4–8 weeks. US visas need months. Start early. For VOA countries (Bali, Dubai, Maldives) — still carry hotel bookings and return tickets.

08

Use International SIM or eSIM

Airalo and Holafly offer affordable global eSIMs from ₹500–₹2,000 for 7 days. No roaming charges. Activate before landing to use navigation immediately on arrival.

09

Photograph Your Luggage

Before checking bags, photograph the contents. Store digital copies of passport, visa, and tickets on Google Drive. Enable location sharing with family for solo trips abroad.
